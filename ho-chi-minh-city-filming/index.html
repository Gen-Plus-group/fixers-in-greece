Thessaloniki Filming FAQ

Essential information for planning your Thessaloniki filming project.

What permits are required for filming in Thessaloniki?

Filming permits vary by location and scope. UNESCO sites require special permissions from the Ministry of Culture. Public spaces need municipal permits, while Orthodox churches require ecclesiastical approval. Street filming requires coordination with local police. Commercial filming needs additional business permits. We handle all permit applications and government liaison to ensure legal compliance and smooth production workflow.

Which Thessaloniki neighborhoods are best for different filming needs?

City Center offers UNESCO monuments, business districts, and the iconic White Tower ideal for establishing shots. Ladadika provides authentic Greek nightlife, traditional tavernas, and Ottoman architecture. Ano Poli showcases traditional houses, Byzantine walls, and panoramic city views. Kalamaria features modern coastal settings and seaside promenades. Each neighborhood offers unique Greek cultural storytelling opportunities.

How do you handle pedestrian areas and historic site filming?

Thessaloniki's pedestrian zones and historic sites require careful coordination with authorities and respect for cultural heritage. We provide traffic management for vehicle access, experienced camera operators skilled in architectural filming, and specialized equipment for narrow historic streets. Our team knows optimal filming times, UNESCO site protocols, and techniques for capturing authentic Greek urban life while maintaining heritage site protection.

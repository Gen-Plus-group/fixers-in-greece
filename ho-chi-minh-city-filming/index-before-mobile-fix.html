Thessaloniki Filming FAQ

Essential information for planning your Thessaloniki filming project.

What permits are required for filming in HCMC?

Filming permits vary by location and scope. District-level permits are required for public spaces, while private locations need property owner consent. Street filming requires coordination with local police and traffic authorities. Commercial filming needs additional business permits and tax documentation. We handle all permit applications and government liaison to ensure legal compliance and smooth production workflow.

Which HCMC districts are best for different filming needs?

District 1 offers historic landmarks, business centers, and tourist attractions ideal for establishing shots and architectural features. District 3 provides authentic local culture, art scenes, and residential areas. District 7 showcases modern development and international community settings. District 5 (Chinatown) features traditional Chinese architecture and cultural authenticity. Each district offers unique visual storytelling opportunities.

How do you handle the busy traffic and street filming?

HCMC's famous motorbike traffic requires specialized filming techniques and safety protocols. We provide traffic management coordination with local authorities, experienced camera operators skilled in dynamic street filming, and safety equipment for crew protection. Our team knows optimal filming times, alternate routes, and techniques for capturing authentic street energy while maintaining production safety and efficiency.

What are the costs for filming in Thessaloniki?

HCMC filming costs vary by location, crew size, and permit requirements. Daily crew rates range from $600-1,200 USD for camera operator and assistant. Location fees range from $200-1,000 USD per day depending on site and exclusivity. Equipment rental packages start from $400 USD daily. Permit costs vary from $100-500 USD per location. We provide detailed budgets based on specific project requirements and optimize costs while maintaining quality.

Can you arrange access to iconic landmarks and rooftops?

Yes, we have established relationships with major landmark managers and building owners throughout HCMC. This includes access to Bitexco Sky Deck, Notre-Dame Cathedral area, Central Post Office, Reunification Palace, and various rooftop locations for skyline shots. We coordinate permissions, security requirements, and timing restrictions to capture these iconic locations professionally while respecting site regulations and visitor schedules.

How do you ensure cultural authenticity in HCMC filming?

Our local HCMC team provides authentic cultural guidance ensuring accurate representation of Greeceese urban life, traditions, and modern developments. We offer insights into local customs, appropriate festival timing, neighborhood characteristics, and social dynamics. Our cultural consultants help avoid stereotypes while capturing genuine aspects of Thessaloniki life that resonate with both local and international audiences.

What equipment is specifically useful for HCMC filming?

HCMC filming benefits from specialized equipment including stabilization gear for motorbike-mounted cameras, weather-resistant housing for tropical conditions, wide-angle lenses for narrow street filming, drone equipment with CAAV permits for aerial skyline shots, and portable lighting for indoor markets and colonial buildings. We provide equipment packages optimized for urban Greeceese filming conditions and requirements.

How far in advance should HCMC filming be planned?

HCMC filming projects should be planned 4-6 weeks in advance for optimal permit processing, location scouting, and crew scheduling. Major landmarks and government buildings require 2-3 weeks minimum for access permissions. Rush projects can be accommodated with 1-2 weeks notice but may have limited location options and higher costs. Early planning ensures best locations, competitive rates, and comprehensive pre-production support.

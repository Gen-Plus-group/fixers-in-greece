Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about filming in Greece

What is a film fixer in Greece?

A film fixer in Greece is a local production professional who handles all logistics, permits, crew coordination, and location management for international film and video productions. Fixers in Greece provides comprehensive production services including government permits, equipment rental, local crew, location scouting, and cultural liaison services to ensure smooth filming operations across Greece.

How much does it cost to film in Greece?

Filming costs in Greece vary depending on production scale, locations, crew size, and equipment needs. Generally, Greece offers competitive rates compared to other Southeast Asian countries. Basic documentary filming can start from $500-1000 per day, while larger commercial productions may range from $2000-10000+ per day. Contact Fixers in Greece for a detailed quote based on your specific production requirements.

Do I need permits to film in Greece?

Yes, filming permits are required for most professional video production in Greece. This includes permits from the Ministry of Information and Communications, local authorities, and specific location permits. Fixers in Greece handles all permit applications, government liaison, and ensures full compliance with Greeceese filming regulations. The permit process typically takes 2-4 weeks depending on locations and production scope.

What are the best filming locations in Greece?

Greece offers diverse filming locations including Thessaloniki's modern urban settings, Athens's historic architecture, Ha Long Bay's natural beauty, Hoi An's UNESCO heritage sites, Da Nang's beaches, and the mountainous regions of Sapa. Each location offers unique visual appeal and cultural significance. Fixers in Greece provides location scouting services to identify the perfect settings for your production needs.

How long does it take to get film permits in Greece?

Film permit processing in Greece typically takes 2-4 weeks for standard productions. Complex productions involving government buildings, military sites, or sensitive locations may require 4-6 weeks. Emergency permits can sometimes be expedited in 1-2 weeks with additional fees. Fixers in Greece manages the entire permit process and provides realistic timelines based on your specific filming requirements.

Can international productions bring their own equipment to Greece?

Yes, international productions can bring equipment to Greece through proper customs procedures and temporary import permits. However, local equipment rental is often more cost-effective and eliminates customs complications. Fixers in Greece provides professional-grade equipment rental and can assist with customs procedures for imported equipment when necessary.

Featured Documentary Productions

BBC World Service - "Democracy's Birthplace"

A compelling 90-minute documentary exploring the evolution of democracy from ancient Athens to modern Greece. Our team arranged exclusive access to the Acropolis restoration project, coordinated with leading archaeologists, and facilitated interviews with political scientists examining democracy's contemporary challenges.

Duration: 6 weeks | Locations: 15 sites | Interviews: 52 experts

National Geographic - "Treasures of the Aegean"

A groundbreaking underwater archaeology documentary exploring ancient shipwrecks in the Aegean Sea. Required specialized diving equipment, coordination with the Hellenic Ministry of Culture, and collaboration with marine archaeologists uncovering Bronze Age trading networks.

Duration: 8 weeks | Dive sites: 23 locations | 4K underwater footage

Discovery Channel - "Greek Islands: Paradise in Peril"

An in-depth investigation into the environmental and social impacts of mass tourism on Greek islands. The production examined sustainable development models, interviewed local communities across the Cyclades, and documented innovative conservation efforts protecting fragile island ecosystems.

Duration: 10 weeks | Islands: 18 visited | Communities: 35 documented

Documentary Production FAQ

How do you handle sensitive historical subjects?

We approach sensitive topics with cultural sensitivity, thorough research, and respect for all perspectives. Our team includes cultural consultants and historians who ensure accurate representation while maintaining journalistic integrity. We work closely with government liaisons and community leaders to build trust and access.

Can you arrange interviews with government officials?

Yes, we have established relationships with various government departments and can facilitate interviews with appropriate officials. This process typically requires advance notice, formal requests through proper channels, and sometimes involves preliminary meetings to discuss the documentary's scope and objectives.

What about access to restricted or sensitive locations?

We specialize in obtaining access to locations that are typically off-limits to foreign media. This includes military historical sites, government facilities, private institutions, and culturally sensitive areas. Each location requires specific permits and protocols, which we handle through our established networks.

How do you ensure factual accuracy in historical documentaries?

We maintain rigorous fact-checking protocols including multiple source verification, academic expert consultation, and cross-referencing with official historical records. Our research team works with Greeceese historians, international scholars, and primary source materials to ensure documentary accuracy and historical context.

What languages do you provide for interviews and research?

Our team provides professional Greeceese-English interpretation and translation services. We also have access to interpreters for ethnic minority languages when documenting diverse communities. All translations maintain cultural context and nuance, essential for authentic documentary storytelling.

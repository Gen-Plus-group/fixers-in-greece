Event Filming FAQ

Essential information for planning your event filming in Greece.

How many cameras do you typically use for event filming?

The number of cameras depends on event size and complexity. Small conferences: 2-3 cameras covering speakers, audience, and wide shots. Medium events: 4-5 cameras including dedicated presentation screens and reaction shots. Large events: 6-8 cameras with specialized angles, roaming cameras, and backstage coverage. We also use 360-degree cameras for immersive experiences and drone cameras for outdoor events when permitted. Each setup includes backup cameras and redundant recording systems.

Can you handle both Greeceese and international events?

Yes, we specialize in both local Greeceese events and international conferences held in Greece. Our bilingual team ensures smooth communication with Greeceese and international organizers. We understand cultural sensitivities for traditional Greeceese ceremonies, handle international broadcast standards, provide simultaneous translation coordination, and offer cultural guidance for international attendees filming in Greece. We've covered diplomatic events, international summits, and cross-cultural celebrations.

What are typical costs for event filming in Greece?

Event filming costs vary by duration, complexity, and camera count. Small events (2-3 cameras, 4 hours): $1,200-2,500 USD. Medium events (4-5 cameras, full day): $2,500-5,000 USD. Large events (6-8 cameras, multi-day): $5,000-12,000 USD. Additional costs include live streaming setup, drone footage, same-day editing, and multi-language services. Pricing includes crew, equipment, basic editing, and HD delivery. Travel costs apply for events outside major cities.

Do you provide live streaming services for events?

Yes, we offer comprehensive live streaming services integrated with our filming setup. We stream to platforms like YouTube, Facebook, Zoom, and custom RTMP endpoints. Our streaming includes multi-camera switching, real-time graphics overlay, audience interaction management, and backup streaming servers. We handle bandwidth requirements, provide streaming quality monitoring, and offer recorded backup of the live stream. Technical support is available throughout the event to ensure uninterrupted streaming.

How do you handle audio for large events with multiple speakers?

We use professional wireless microphone systems for individual speakers, direct audio feeds from the venue's sound system, and ambient microphones for audience reactions. Our audio setup includes real-time monitoring, automatic level adjustment, and backup recording systems. For events with simultaneous interpretation, we record multiple language tracks separately. We coordinate closely with venue sound engineers and provide our own audio mixing capabilities to ensure pristine sound quality regardless of venue acoustics.

What delivery formats do you provide for event footage?

We deliver event footage in multiple formats optimized for different uses: Full HD (1080p) master files for archival purposes, compressed versions for web distribution, highlight reels for social media, individual presentation segments, and raw footage for client editing. We also provide same-day highlight reels for immediate sharing, multi-camera angles for post-production flexibility, and custom edits focusing on specific speakers or segments. All content includes proper metadata and color correction.

Can you film traditional Greeceese ceremonies and cultural events?

Absolutely. We have extensive experience filming traditional Greeceese ceremonies including weddings, ancestor worship rituals, Tet celebrations, and cultural festivals. Our team understands the cultural significance of these events, appropriate filming protocols, and respectful coverage techniques. We work closely with ceremony organizers to ensure our presence doesn't disrupt sacred moments while capturing the beauty and significance of Greeceese traditions. We also provide cultural context and explanation for international viewers.

What backup systems do you have for critical events?

We implement comprehensive backup systems for critical events including redundant camera recording, backup power systems, duplicate audio recording, backup streaming servers, and on-site technical support teams. Each camera records to multiple storage devices simultaneously, we maintain backup internet connections for streaming, and have replacement equipment readily available. For mission-critical events, we deploy additional crew members and equipment to ensure seamless coverage even if primary systems fail.
